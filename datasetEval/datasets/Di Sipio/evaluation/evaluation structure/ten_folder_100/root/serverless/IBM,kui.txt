By combining the power of familiar CLIs\nwith visualizations in high-impact areas, Kui enables you to\nmanipulate complex JSON and YAML data models, integrate disparate\ntooling, and provides quick access to aggregate views of operational\ndata.\n\n## Quick Install\n\nThe fastest option is to install a prebuilt release.\n\n[Kui-MacOS.tar.bz2](https://macos-tarball.kui-shell.org) **|** [Kui-Linux.zip](https://linux-zip.kui-shell.org)\n\nVisit the [Kui Installation Guide](docs/installation.md)\nfor installation details and alternative installation options.\n\n## Contributing\n\nIf you want to help, please take a look at our [guidelines](CONTRIBUTING.md) and [developer guide](docs/dev/README.md).\n\n## The Kui Experience\n\n[![Kui screenshot](docs/readme/images/kubectl-examples.jpg)](https://youtu.be/jcV0csyzGdY)\n\nClick the image to see [Kui in\nmotion](https://youtu.be/jcV0csyzGdY). For more of the backstory of\nKui, read on!\n\n## This is a CLI, with Visualizations on the Side\n\nKui uses [Electron](https://electronjs.org) to provide you with an\naugmented but CLI-focused development experience. By using Electron,\nthe same experience carries over, from local development on your\nlaptop, to a browser-based experience.\n\nWhen running locally, you will have access to your filesystem and your\nfavorite terminal and text editor. To help with complex data, Kui\noffers a suite of **visualizations**. You can gracefully flip between\nthe terminal and these visualizations, without having to switch to\nyour browser, log in, wait for pages to load, and navigate through\ncomplex menu structures.\n\nFor example, to see a visual summary of your Kubernetes pods, issue\nthis command from your favorite terminal:\n\n| Example Command             | Thumbnail of Output                                                                               |\n| :-------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |\n| `kubectl kui get pods --ui` | [![](docs/readme/images/kubectl-get-pods-thumbnail.jpg)](docs/readme/images/kubectl-get-pods.png) |\n\nWithout the `--ui` option, Kui will display the output in your\nterminal directly; you will observe that the output is identical to\nthat of `kubectl`, with the possible addition of syntax\ncoloration. With Kui, you have the power to navigate between these\nmodes in a graceful and flexible manner.\n\n<a href="https://ibm.box.com/shared/static/55gasbz9fc40qrg43iq4b8t1uckupft4.gif">\n    <img align="right" alt="kubectl get pods drilldown animated gif" src="docs/readme/images/kubectl-get-pods-thumbnail.gif"></img>\n</a>\n\nNow try clicking on a row. You should see the "sidecar" sweep in from\nthe right. This split screen mode allows you to drill down to the\ndetails of your pods. You can click on one, then another, for rapid\ninspection of several of your resources. The animated gif should give\nyou a sense of the possibilities (click on it to see a larger\nversion).\n\n## Kui as a Tool Platform\n\nKui can form the basis for delivering CLI-driven GUI experiences.
