It\'s written in Python. Persepolis is a sample of free and open source software. It\'s developed for GNU/Linux distributions, BSDs, MacOS, and Microsoft Windows.  \n\n### **Features**\n- Multi-segment downloading\n- Scheduling downloads\n- Download queuing\n- Finding and downloading video from Youtube, Vimeo, DailyMotion, etc.  \n- and many more!\n\n### **FAQ**\n> **Where does the name come from?**\n\n> > [From Wikipedia:](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persepolis) Persepolis (Greek: \xce\xa0\xce\xb5\xcf\x81\xcf\x83\xce\xad\xcf\x80\xce\xbf\xce\xbb\xce\xb9\xcf\x82 Pers\xc3\xa9polis; "the Persian city") or Parsa (Old Persian: \xf0\x90\x8e\xb1\xf0\x90\x8e\xa0\xf0\x90\x8e\xbc\xf0\x90\x8e\xbf P\xc4\x81rsa; "Persia"), \nalso known as Takht-e-Jamshid (Persian: \xd8\xaa\xd8\xae\xd8\xaa \xd8\xac\xd9\x85\xd8\xb4\xd9\x8a\xd8\xaf\xe2\x80\x8e\xe2\x80\x8e Taxt e Jam\xc5\xa1id; "Throne of Jamshid"),\nwas the ceremonial capital of the Achaemenid Empire (ca. 550\xe2\x80\x93330 BC).\nPersepolis is situated 60 km northeast of the city of Shiraz in Fars Province, Iran.
