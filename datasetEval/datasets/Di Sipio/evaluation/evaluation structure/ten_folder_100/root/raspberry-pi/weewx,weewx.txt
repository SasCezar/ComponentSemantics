b'# [WeeWX](http://www.weewx.com)\n*Open source software for your weather station*\n\n## Description\n\nThe WeeWX weather system is written in Python and runs on Linux, MacOSX,\nSolaris, and *BSD.  It runs exceptionally well on a Raspberry Pi.  It generates\nplots, HTML pages, and monthly and yearly summary reports, which can be\nuploaded to a web server. Thousands of users worldwide!\n\nSee the WeeWX website for [examples](http://weewx.com/showcase.html) of web\nsites generated by WeeWX, and a [map](http://weewx.com/stations.html) of\nstations using WeeWX.\n\n* Robust and hard-to-crash\n* Designed with the enthusiast in mind\n* Simple, easy to understand internal design that is easily extended (Python skills recommended)\n* Growing ecosystem of 3rd party extensions\n* Internationalized language support\n* Localized date/time support\n* Support for US and metric units\n* Support for multiple skins\n* Support sqlite and MySQL\n* Extensive almanac information\n* Uploads to your website via FTP, FTPS, or rsync\n* Uploads to online weather services\n\nSupport for many online weather services, including:\n\n* The Weather Underground\n* CWOP\n* PWSweather\n* WOW\n* AWEKAS\n* Open Weathermap\n* WeatherBug\n* Weather Cloud\n* Wetter\n* Windfinder\n\nSupport for many data aggregation services, including:\n\n* EmonCMS\n* Graphite\n* InfluxDB\n* MQTT\n* Smart Energy Groups\n* Thingspeak\n* Twitter\n* Xively\n\nSupport for over 70 types of hardware including, but not limited to:\n\n* Davis Vantage Pro, Pro2, Vue, Envoy;\n* Oregon Scientific WMR100, WMR200, WMR300, WMR9x8, and other variants;\n* Oregon Scientific LW300/LW301/LW302;\n* Fine Offset WH10xx, WH20xx, and WH30xx series (including Ambient, Elecsa, Maplin, Tycon, Watson, and others);\n* Fine Offset WH23xx, WH4000 (including Tycon TP2700, MiSol WH2310);\n* Fine Offset WH2600, HP1000 (including Ambient Observer, Aercus WeatherSleuth, XC0422);\n* LaCrosse WS-23XX and WS-28XX (including TFA);\n* LaCrosse GW1000U bridge;\n* Hideki TE923, TE831, TE838, DV928 (including TFA, Cresta, Honeywell, and others);\n* PeetBros Ultimeter;\n* RainWise CC3000 and MKIII;\n* AcuRite 5-in-1 via USB console or bridge;\n* Argent Data Systems WS1;\n* KlimaLogg Pro;\n* New Mountain;\n* AirMar 150WX;\n* Texas Weather Instruments;\n* Dyacon;\n* Meteostick;\n* Ventus W820;\n* Si1000 radio receiver;\n* Software Defined Radio (SDR);\n* One-wire (including Inspeed, ADS, AAG, Hobby-Boards).\n\nSee the [hardware list](http://www.weewx.com/hardware.html) for a complete list\nof
