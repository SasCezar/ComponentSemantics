b"# em: the cli emoji keyboard\xe2\x84\xa2\n\n**Emoji your friends and colleagues from the comfort of your own\nterminal.**\n\n**em** is a nifty command-line utility for referencing emoji characters\nby name. Provide the names of a few emoji, and those lucky chosen emojis\nwill be displayed in your terminal, then copied to your clipboard.\nAutomagically.\n\nEmoji can be also searched by both categories and aspects.\n\n![Screenshot of em command-line\ninterface.](http://f.cl.ly/items/0P3e11201W1o420O1N1S/Screen%20Shot%202016-07-25%20at%202.00.32%20AM.png)\n\n## Example Usage\n\nLet's serve some delicious cake:\n\n    $ em sparkles cake sparkles\n    Copied! \xe2\x9c\xa8\xf0\x9f\x8d\xb0\xe2\x9c\xa8\n\nLet's skip the copying (for scripts):\n\n    $ em 'chocolate bar' --no-copy\n    \xf0\x9f\x8d\xab\n\nLet's find some emoji, by color:\n\n    $ em -s red\n    \xf0\x9f\x9a\x97  car\n    \xf0\x9f\x8e\xb4  flower_playing_cards\n    \xf0\x9f\x91\xb9  japanese_ogre\n    \xf0\x9f\x91\xba  japanese_goblin\n\n## Installation\n\nAt this time, **em** requires Python and pip:\n\n    $ pip install em-keyboard\n\nThat's it!\n\n## Tests\n\nIf you wanna develop, you might want to write and run tests:\n\n    $ pip install tox\n    $ tox\n\n## Have fun!\n\n\xe2\x9c\xa8\xf0\x9f\x8d\xb0\xe2\x9c\xa8\n"
